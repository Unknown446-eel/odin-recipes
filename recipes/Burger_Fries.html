Description

Big, bold, and unapologetically messy. Think half-pound patties, charred edges, juicy centers, and toppings that mean business: thick-cut bacon, melty cheddar, grilled onions, maybe a fried egg if you’re feeling outlaw. Served with crispy, hand-cut fries—golden outside, fluffy inside—often dusted with a touch of seasoned salt or smoky paprika.

Ingredients

Burger

  • 2 lb (900 g) 80/20 ground beef
  • 1½ tsp kosher salt
  • 1 tsp coarse black pepper
  • 4 slices thick-cut cheddar
  • 4 large sturdy burger buns
  • 8 slices thick-cut bacon
  • 1 large onion, sliced into ½-inch rings
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • your prefered toppings

Fries

  • 4 large russet potatoes
  • 2–3 Tbsp cornstarch
  • 1½ tsp kosher salt
  • 1½ tsp course black pepper
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika

Steps

  1. Cut potatoes into ¼-inch sticks.
  2. Rinse in cold water until the water runs mostly clear.
  3. Soak in a large bowl of cold water for at least 30 minutes.
  4. Drain and pat completely dry with a clean towel.
  5. Divide beef into 4 equal portions.
  6. Gently form into patties about 1 inch thick, making a small dimple in the center.
  7. Season just before cooking with salt and pepper.
  8. Heat oil to 325°F.
  9. Fry potatoes in small batches for 4–5 minutes until pale and softened.
  10. Drain on a wire rack or paper towels.
  11. Heat skillet until smoking. Cook patties 3–4 minutes per side. Add cheese, cover for melt.
  12. Spread butter on cut sides and toast on the grill/griddle until golden.
  13. Raise oil to 375°F (191°C).
  14. Fry potatoes until deep golden and crisp.
  15. Drain, toss with salt (and paprika if using).
  16. Assemble Burger and Fries
